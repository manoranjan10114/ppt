# CEO Business Plan 2026–27 — Presentation Guide

> How to present each slide, what to say, and what to emphasize. Keep it conversational, data-led, and forward-looking.

---

## Slide 01 — Cover: CEO Business Plan 2026–27

**What to say:**
Open with the scale of the opportunity, not the problem. Set the room's expectation that this is a transformation plan, not a status update.

**Key talking points:**

- We serve 1,800 hotels today — that's a strong distribution base
- Current revenue is ₹2.4 Cr with an ARPU of just ₹1,100/month — the monetization is the gap, not the market
- Annual churn is sitting at 30% — 1 in 3 customers walking out every year
- The target is $15M (~₹125 Cr) by FY 2026–27 — this plan is about how we get there

**Tone:** Confident. This is a plan with a clear destination.

---

## Slide 02 — Reality Check: Current State

**What to say:**
Be honest and direct. Don't soften the numbers — the room needs to feel the urgency before they'll buy into the solution.

**Key talking points:**

- Revenue achieved ₹2.4 Cr vs ₹2.97 Cr target — 81% achievement, ₹56L gap
- 30% churn means 535 customers lost — that's ₹70L in revenue walking out the door annually
- Sales conversion at 8.15% is half the industry benchmark of 15–25%
- ARPU of ₹13K/year is extremely low for a multi-product platform
- Three signals to call out: new sales are declining YoY, renewals are holding stable, and commission revenue is severely underutilized

**Pause here:** "We have the distribution. We have the product. What we don't have yet is the monetization engine."

---

## Slide 03 — ARPU Deep Dive: We're Undercharging

**What to say:**
This is the most important financial insight in the deck. Make the math land.

**Key talking points:**

- Current ARPU: ₹1,100/month — that's ₹2.4 Cr divided across 1,800 hotels
- Realistic target: ₹2,500–₹3,000/month — a 2.5–3x increase, not a moonshot
- At target ARPU with the same 1,800 customers, revenue jumps to ₹54–65 Cr — zero new acquisition needed
- Why it's low: 30+ confusing pricing plans, no upgrade path, features not monetized individually, bundles underpriced
- How we fix it: 3 clean tiers, add-on marketplace, drive direct bookings, AI upsell triggers

**Key line to land:** "The biggest lever for growth is extracting more value from the 1,800 customers we already have — not acquiring new ones."

---

## Slide 04 — Sales Funnel: Where We Lose Deals

**What to say:**
Walk the funnel top to bottom. The story here is that the product isn't the problem — the process is.

**Key talking points:**

- 4,513 leads entered the funnel
- Only 969 (21%) got to a demo — 79% dropped before we even showed the product
- Of those who saw a demo, ~38% converted to a sale — that's actually strong
- Overall conversion: 8.15% — the problem is entirely top-of-funnel
- The fix is not more leads — it's better qualification and follow-up on the leads we already have

**Actions to highlight:**

- AI lead scoring to prioritize high-intent leads
- Automated follow-up sequences so no lead goes cold
- Demo script optimization to improve show rate
- Sales scripting and objection handling to close faster

**Key line:** "The product sells itself once someone sees it. Our job is to get more people to the demo."

---

## Slide 05 — Churn Analysis: The ₹70L Leak

**What to say:**
Frame churn as a revenue problem, not a customer satisfaction problem. Put a rupee number on it.

**Key talking points:**

- 535 customers lost out of 1,800 — that's 29.7% churn, nearly 1 in 3
- Direct revenue impact: ₹70L lost every year just from churn
- Root causes are systemic, not random: low product usage post-onboarding, poor onboarding experience, weak engagement between renewals, price vs value mismatch, support gaps
- Every root cause has a fix — this is a solvable problem

**Transition:** "Now let's look at exactly how CSM can be the engine that stops this leak."

---

## Slide 06 — CSM Retention Strategy: Win Retention via CSM Excellence

**What to say:**
This is the operational heart of the retention plan. Make it concrete — CSMs need to know exactly what changes.

**Key talking points:**

- Cutting churn from 30% → 15% recovers ₹35L+/year — highest ROI action with zero acquisition spend
- Four pillars of the CSM playbook:
  1. **Account Health Scoring** — AI-driven Red/Amber/Green risk segmentation, churn alerts 30–45 days before renewal, live CSM dashboard
  2. **Proactive Engagement Playbook** — structured 30-day onboarding, monthly check-ins, automated touchpoints, QBRs for top accounts
  3. **Renewal & Upsell Engine** — renewal pipeline tracked 90 days out, CSM time prioritized on high-risk accounts, upsell triggers tied to usage milestones, target 20%+ upsell rate
  4. **CSM Performance Metrics** — each CSM owns churn %, renewal rate, upsell %; incentives tied to retention not just new sales

**Key line:** "CSM is not a support function — it's a revenue protection function."

---

## Slide 07 — Growth Framework: 4 Strategic Pillars

**What to say:**
Zoom out from the operational detail and show the full strategic picture. This is the "here's how everything connects" slide.

**Key talking points:**

- Pillar 1 — Revenue Protection: AI health scoring, churn alerts, renewal automation. Target: churn 30% → 15%
- Pillar 2 — Revenue Expansion: ARPU ₹1K → ₹2.5–3K, 3 pricing tiers, add-on marketplace, drive direct bookings and cut OTA dependency (18–25% commission)
- Pillar 3 — Upsell Engine: Monetize existing 1,800 hotels, plan upgrade campaigns, BE & CRM/PMS upsell, AI-powered triggers. Target: +₹1.6 Cr from upsell
- Pillar 4 — Market Expansion: Thailand 50 clients in Q1, Hindi & regional languages, Tier 2/3 city penetration. Target: +₹3 Cr new customers

**Key line:** "These four pillars work together — protect what we have, expand what we charge, upsell what we've built, and grow where we haven't been."

---

## Slide 08 — Pricing Revamp: Simplify to Scale

**What to say:**
The current pricing is a mess — 30+ plans is not a pricing strategy, it's a pricing accident. Show the clean solution.

**Key talking points:**

- Current state: 30+ pricing variations causing confusion, no clear upgrade path, weak upsell structure
- New structure: 3 clean tiers
  - **Basic ₹1K/mo** — Channel Manager, Basic PMS, email support
  - **Growth ₹2K/mo** — Booking Engine (direct booking focus), CRM, AI health alerts
  - **Premium ₹3K/mo** — Full PMS, Revenue Management, Dedicated CSM, direct booking analytics
- The strategic goal of the Booking Engine tier: every hotel on Growth or Premium shifts guests from OTA (18–25% commission) to direct (0% commission)
- This is how we help hotels save money while we grow ARPU — a genuine win-win

**Key line:** "Simpler pricing means faster decisions, clearer upgrades, and a product that sells itself."

---

## Slide 09 — AI Transformation: AI-Led Efficiency

**What to say:**
AI is not a buzzword here — it's the mechanism that lets us scale without proportional headcount growth.

**Key talking points:**

- Goal: 2–3x efficiency without additional hiring
- **Sales AI**: Lead scoring, automated follow-ups, demo assistant — target conversion 8% → 15%+
- **CSM AI**: Real-time account health score, 30–45 day churn prediction, automated renewal engine, task prioritization
- **Support AI**: AI chatbot handling 60% of tickets, SLA breach alerts, self-serve knowledge base, sub-15 sec OTA sync monitoring
- Trust fix: Zero overbooking goal, real-time sync alerts, sync health dashboard — this directly supports the direct booking strategy

**Key line:** "AI doesn't replace our team — it makes every person on the team 2–3x more effective."

---

## Slide 10 — Revenue Projection: Path to ₹15–20 Cr

**What to say:**
Show the math. Break down where the revenue comes from so it feels achievable, not aspirational.

**Key talking points:**

- ARPU increase alone: ₹5 Cr
- Upsell from existing base: ₹1.6 Cr
- New customer acquisition: ₹3 Cr
- Direct booking revenue (hotels shifting from OTA to direct): ₹5–10 Cr
- Realistic total: ₹15–20 Cr | Stretch: ₹30–50 Cr | Transformational: ₹100 Cr+

**CEO Truth to land hard:** "$15M requires structural transformation, not incremental growth — pricing, retention, and shifting hotels from OTA dependency to direct bookings must all move together."

---

## Slide 11 — Execution Roadmap: Quarterly Playbook

**What to say:**
Make it real with timelines. Show that this isn't a vision document — it's a plan with quarters and owners.

**Key talking points:**

- **Q1**: AI churn system live, pricing revamp to 3 tiers, Thailand setup (50 clients), CSM dashboard launch, renewal automation v1
- **Q2**: Upsell campaigns live, AI lead scoring deployed, Booking Engine push, Hindi localisation, Support AI bot beta
- **Q3**: Add-on marketplace live, sales team scaling, regional language rollout, direct booking adoption push, Tier 2/3 India push
- **Q4**: Full AI optimisation, revenue push sprint, Thailand scale to 200, annual review & reset, FY 2027–28 planning

**Key line:** "Q1 is about fixing the foundation. Q2–Q3 is about scaling what works. Q4 is about locking in the new baseline."

---

## Slide 12 — Key Metrics: What Gets Measured

**What to say:**
Every strategy lives or dies by what you track. This is the weekly dashboard the leadership team owns.

**Key talking points:**

- MRR: ₹20L → ₹1.25 Cr+ (Owner: CEO / Finance)
- ARPU/Month: ₹1,100 → ₹2,500–₹3,000 (Owner: Product / CSM)
- Churn Rate: 30% → 15% (Owner: CSM Lead)
- Sales Conversion: 8.15% → 15–20% (Owner: Sales VP)
- Upsell Rate: ~0% → 20%+ (Owner: CSM + Product)
- Direct Booking Share: Underutilised → 20–30% of total (Owner: Sales / BE Team)

**Key line:** "If these six numbers move in the right direction, the revenue target takes care of itself."

---

## Slide 13 — Risks & Mitigation

**What to say:**
Show you've thought about what can go wrong. Confidence comes from having a plan B.

**Key talking points:**

- **High churn continues** → AI health scoring + CSM system + renewal playbook already in motion from Q1
- **Low sales conversion** → Funnel optimization + AI lead scoring + scripting — process fix, not a people fix
- **Pricing resistance** → Clear value communication, ROI calculators, trial periods to reduce friction
- **Team bandwidth** → AI automation is the answer — 2–3x output without new hires

**Key line:** "Every risk on this list has a mitigation that's already part of the plan."

---

## Slide 14 — Closing

**What to say:**
End with conviction. Bring it back to the core truth and leave the room energized.

**Key talking points:**

- "We are not limited by product or market — we are limited by monetization, retention, and execution."
- Growth will come from extracting more value from the 1,800 customers we already have — not just acquiring new ones
- The infrastructure exists. The opportunity is clear. The plan is in front of you.

**Close with the five commitments on screen:**

- Churn: 30% → 15%
- ARPU: ₹1K → ₹2.5–3K/mo
- Conversion: 8% → 20%
- Target: ₹15–20 Cr
- Timeline: FY 2026–27

**Final line:** "The question isn't whether this is possible. The question is how fast we move."

---

> **Presentation tips:**
>
> - Total slides: 14 — budget ~3–4 minutes per slide for a 45–60 min session
> - Slides 02–05 are the problem statement — don't rush them, the audience needs to feel the pain
> - Slides 06–09 are the solution — be specific, avoid vague language
> - Slides 10–13 are the commitment — speak with ownership, not hedging
> - Use keyboard arrows or spacebar to navigate during the live presentation

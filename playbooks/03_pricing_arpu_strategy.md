# PLAYBOOK 03 — Pricing & ARPU Strategy

> Owner: Product + CEO + Finance
> Target: ARPU ₹1,100/mo → ₹2,500–₹3,000/mo · Timeline: Q1

---

## WHY PRICING IS THE #1 LEVER

We have 1,800 hotels. Even if we don't add a single new customer, moving ARPU from ₹1,100 to ₹2,500 means revenue goes from ₹2.4 Cr to ₹5.4 Cr — more than doubled. Pricing is not a "nice to have" — it's the single biggest revenue lever we control.

But pricing can't be arbitrary. It must be tied to value the hotel actually receives.

---

## HOW TO DECIDE THE 3 TIERS

### Principle: Price = Value Delivered, Not Cost of Features

Hotels don't care about features. They care about:

1. **More bookings** (especially direct bookings)
2. **Less money lost to OTAs** (18–25% commission saved)
3. **Less manual work** (automation)
4. **No overbookings** (trust)

Each tier should deliver progressively more of these outcomes.

### Tier Design Framework

|                            | Basic (₹1K/mo)                                                          | Growth (₹2K/mo)                                              | Premium (₹3K/mo)                                                  |

### How to Validate Pricing Before Launch

**Step 1: Analyze current customer distribution**

Pull data on all 1,800 customers:

| Data Point                    | Why                                                   |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Current plan name and price   | Understand what they're paying today                  |
| Features they actually use    | Know which tier they'd naturally fit into             |
| Hotel size (rooms)            | Correlates with willingness to pay                    |
| Location (Tier 1/2/3)         | Affects price sensitivity                             |
| Booking Engine usage (if any) | Identifies who's already getting direct booking value |
| Monthly revenue per customer  | Current ARPU distribution                             |

**Step 2: Map every customer to a new tier**

| Current Price    | Likely New Tier   | Migration Impact                          |
| ---------------- | ----------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| < ₹800/mo        | Basic (₹1K)       | Price increase — need value justification |
| ₹800–₹1,500/mo   | Basic or Growth   | Depends on features used                  |
| ₹1,500–₹2,500/mo | Growth (₹2K)      | Neutral or slight adjustment              |
| ₹2,500–₹4,000/mo | Growth or Premium | Depends on features used                  |
| > ₹4,000/mo      | Premium (₹3K)     | Price decrease — grandfather or adjust    |

**Step 3: Customer interviews (2 weeks)**

Talk to 30–50 hotels across segments:

- 10 small hotels (< 20 rooms): "Would you pay ₹1K/mo for [Basic features]?"
- 15 mid-size hotels (20–50 rooms): "Would you pay ₹2K/mo if it included a Booking Engine that saves you OTA commissions?"
- 10 large hotels (50+ rooms): "Would you pay ₹3K/mo for full PMS + dedicated CSM + direct booking analytics?"
- 10 churned hotels: "What price point would have kept you?"
- 5 competitor users: "What are you paying now and what's missing?"

**Key questions to ask:**

- "How much do you pay OTAs in commissions per month?" (establishes the savings baseline)
- "If we could get you 20% of your bookings as direct, saving you ₹[X]/month, would ₹2K/month feel fair?"
- "What features would make you upgrade from Basic to Growth?"
- "What would make you leave us for a competitor?"

**Step 4: Price sensitivity analysis**

For each tier, test:

- Would 80%+ of target segment say "yes" at this price? → Price is right
- Would 50–80% say "yes"? → Price might be slightly high, consider launch discount
- Would < 50% say "yes"? → Rethink the tier value or price

### Migration Strategy for Existing 1,800 Customers

**This is the hardest part.** Getting pricing right for new customers is easy. Migrating existing customers without causing churn is the challenge.

#### Option A: Gradual Migration (Recommended)

| Phase   | Timeline  | Action                                                                                                              |
| ------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Phase 1 | Month 1   | Announce new pricing. All NEW customers go on new tiers immediately.                                                |
| Phase 2 | Month 2–3 | Existing customers get a "pricing preview" email: "Here's your new tier, here's what you get, here's what changes." |
| Phase 3 | Month 3–4 | CSMs have 1:1 conversations with every customer about their new tier. Handle objections.                            |
| Phase 4 | Month 4–6 | Migration happens at each customer's renewal date. No mid-contract changes.                                         |
| Phase 5 | Month 6+  | All customers on new tiers. Old plans sunset.                                                                       |

#### Migration Rules

| Scenario                                   | Rule                                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Customer currently pays LESS than new tier | Migrate at renewal. Offer 1 month free as transition incentive. CSM explains added value.                                                        |
| Customer currently pays SAME as new tier   | Seamless migration. Highlight any new features they're getting.                                                                                  |
| Customer currently pays MORE than new tier | Migrate immediately — they get a price reduction. Use this as a goodwill moment: "We've simplified pricing and you're actually paying less now." |
| Customer on a custom/enterprise deal       | Keep custom pricing. Review at next renewal.                                                                                                     |

#### Communication Plan

| Audience                        | Channel                  | Message                                                                            |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| All customers                   | Email                    | "We're simplifying our pricing to 3 clear plans. Here's what it means for you."    |
| High-value customers            | CSM call                 | Personal walkthrough of their new tier, features, and any price change             |
| At-risk customers (health < 70) | CSM call + special offer | "We're giving you 2 months free on your new plan as a thank you for being with us" |
| Churned hotels                  | Winback email            | "We've restructured pricing — plans now start at ₹1K/mo" (ties into Playbook 01)   |

### Add-on Marketplace (Q3)

Beyond the 3 tiers, add-ons let customers pay for specific features without upgrading.

**Potential add-ons:**

| Add-on                                     | Price (est.) | Available to | Rationale                         |
| ------------------------------------------ | ------------ | ------------ | --------------------------------- |
| Extra OTA connections (beyond core)        | ₹200–500/mo  | Basic+       | Some hotels need niche OTAs       |
| White-label booking page                   | ₹300/mo      | Growth+      | Branded direct booking experience |
| Advanced analytics                         | ₹500/mo      | Growth+      | Deep revenue insights             |
| Multi-property management                  | ₹500–1K/mo   | Growth+      | Hotel chains                      |
| Guest communication (automated emails/SMS) | ₹300/mo      | Growth+      | Pre/post-stay engagement          |
| Payment gateway integration                | ₹200/mo      | Growth+      | Direct payment collection         |

**How to decide which add-ons to build first:**

1. Survey 200 customers: "Which of these would you pay extra for?"
2. Analyze support tickets: "What features are most requested?"
3. Analyze usage data: "What features do power users use that others don't?"
4. Competitive analysis: "What do competitors charge extra for?"

### Annual vs Monthly Pricing

| Option  | Monthly | Annual (per month) | Savings       |
| ------- | ------- | ------------------ | ------------- |
| Basic   | ₹1,000  | ₹833 (₹10K/year)   | 2 months free |
| Growth  | ₹2,000  | ₹1,667 (₹20K/year) | 2 months free |
| Premium | ₹3,000  | ₹2,500 (₹30K/year) | 2 months free |

**Push annual aggressively:**

- Reduces churn (committed for 12 months)
- Improves cash flow
- CSMs should default to annual in every conversation
- Offer: "Pay annually and save 2 months — that's ₹[X] back in your pocket"

## REVENUE IMPACT MODEL

| Scenario                           | Assumption   | Revenue       |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------- |
| All 1,800 stay on Basic (₹1K)      | Worst case   | ₹2.16 Cr/year |
| 60% Basic, 30% Growth, 10% Premium | Conservative | ₹3.24 Cr/year |
| 40% Basic, 40% Growth, 20% Premium | Target       | ₹4.32 Cr/year |
| 30% Basic, 40% Growth, 30% Premium | Optimistic   | ₹4.97 Cr/year |

**Current revenue: ₹2.4 Cr.** Even the conservative scenario (60/30/10 split) gets us to ₹3.24 Cr — a 35% increase from pricing alone.

**Target distribution: 40% Basic / 40% Growth / 20% Premium** → ₹4.32 Cr/year = 80% revenue increase.

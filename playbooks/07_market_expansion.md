# PLAYBOOK 07 — Market Expansion

> Owner: CEO + Sales VP
> Target: +₹3 Cr from new customers · Thailand: 50 → 200 clients · India Tier 2/3 penetration · Timeline: Q1–Q4

---

## TWO EXPANSION FRONTS

1. **International: Thailand** — new market, new revenue stream, proof of scalability
2. **Domestic: Tier 2/3 India** — massive untapped market, lower acquisition cost, language localization needed

---

## FRONT 1: THAILAND MARKET ENTRY

### Why Thailand

| Factor            | Detail                                                         |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hotel market size | 30,000+ hotels, growing tourism market                         |
| Tech adoption     | Low penetration of hotel tech — similar to India 5 years ago   |
| Competition       | Fewer established players than India                           |
| Language          | Thai + English (tourism-heavy)                                 |
| Payment           | Local payment gateways needed (PromptPay, Thai bank transfers) |

### Phase 1: Setup (Q1, Weeks 1–8)

| Week     | Action                                                                                                          | Owner                 |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Week 1–2 | Market research: validate demand, identify target hotel segments (budget, mid-range, boutique), map competitors | CEO + Sales VP        |
| Week 2–3 | Legal: decide entity structure (own entity vs local partner). Start registration if own entity.                 | CEO + Legal           |
| Week 3–4 | Product localization: Thai language UI, Thai Baht currency, local payment gateway integration                   | Product + Engineering |
| Week 4–5 | Hire/partner: 1 local sales person or partner agency. Must speak Thai + English.                                | Sales VP + HR         |
| Week 5–6 | Pricing: localize pricing for Thai market (may be different from India based on purchasing power)               | CEO + Finance         |
| Week 6–8 | First 10 pilot hotels: onboard manually, learn what works, iterate                                              | Local sales + CSM     |

### Phase 2: Scale to 50 (Q1–Q2)

| Action           | Detail                                                                                                 |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Sales channel    | Local sales person + digital marketing (Google Ads in Thai, Facebook/Instagram)                        |
| Onboarding       | Dedicated Thai-speaking onboarding support (can be remote from India if Thai-speaking staff available) |
| Support          | Thai-language support during Thai business hours                                                       |
| Partnerships     | Partner with Thai hotel associations, tourism boards, local tech communities                           |
| Referral program | Existing Thai customers refer others → get 1 month free                                                |

### Phase 3: Scale to 200 (Q3–Q4)

| Action                  | Detail                                                                            |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hire local team         | 2–3 sales + 1 CSM based in Thailand                                               |
| Localize marketing      | Thai-language website, case studies from Thai hotels, local social media presence |
| Product iteration       | Incorporate feedback from first 50 Thai hotels into product                       |
| Expand OTA integrations | Add Thailand-specific OTAs (Agoda prominence, local platforms)                    |

### Thailand Pricing Strategy

| Approach                                          | Pros                                                 | Cons                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Same as India (₹1K/₹2K/₹3K converted to THB)      | Simple, consistent                                   | May be too expensive or too cheap for Thai market |
| Localized pricing (based on Thai hotel economics) | Better market fit                                    | Complexity, potential arbitrage                   |
| **Recommended: Localized**                        | Price based on Thai hotel ADR and willingness to pay | Need market research first                        |

**Estimated Thai pricing (to validate):**

| Tier    | India Price         | Thai Price (est.)  | Rationale                             |
| ------- | ------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------- |
| Basic   | ₹1K/mo (~450 THB)   | 500–800 THB/mo     | Slightly higher due to smaller market |
| Growth  | ₹2K/mo (~900 THB)   | 1,000–1,500 THB/mo | BE value proposition same             |
| Premium | ₹3K/mo (~1,350 THB) | 1,500–2,500 THB/mo | Full suite                            |

## FRONT 2: INDIA TIER 2/3 CITY PENETRATION

### Why Tier 2/3

| Factor            | Detail                                                 |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Market size       | 50,000+ hotels in Tier 2/3 cities, most with zero tech |
| Competition       | Very low — most hotel tech companies focus on Tier 1   |
| Price sensitivity | Higher — need affordable entry point                   |
| Language          | Hindi + regional languages critical                    |
| Decision maker    | Often the owner directly — shorter sales cycle         |
| Tech readiness    | Smartphone-first, may not have desktop/laptop          |

### Target Cities (Phase 1)

| Region | Cities                                               | Estimated Hotels |
| ------ | ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------------- |
| North  | Jaipur, Lucknow, Varanasi, Amritsar, Dehradun        | 5,000+           |
| West   | Nashik, Indore, Udaipur, Ahmedabad (non-metro areas) | 4,000+           |
| South  | Mysore, Coimbatore, Vizag, Madurai, Trivandrum       | 5,000+           |
| East   | Bhubaneswar, Ranchi, Guwahati, Siliguri              | 3,000+           |

### Localization Requirements

| Requirement                                           | Priority | Timeline |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | -------- | -------- |
| Hindi UI                                              | P0       | Q2       |
| Hindi onboarding materials (videos, guides)           | P0       | Q2       |
| Hindi support (chat + phone)                          | P0       | Q2       |
| Regional languages (Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada)  | P2       | Q3       |
| Mobile-first experience (product works well on phone) | P1       | Q2       |
| Simplified onboarding (less tech-savvy users)         | P1       | Q2       |

### Go-to-Market Strategy for Tier 2/3

| Channel            | Approach                                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Digital ads        | Google Ads + Facebook/Instagram in Hindi targeting hotel owners in Tier 2/3 cities               |
| WhatsApp marketing | WhatsApp broadcasts to hotel owner groups (very common in Tier 2/3)                              |
| Local partnerships | Hotel associations, tourism departments, local business chambers                                 |
| Field sales        | 2–3 field sales reps covering key regions. In-person demos at the hotel.                         |
| Referral program   | "Refer a hotel → both get 1 month free"                                                          |
| YouTube            | Hindi product demo videos, customer testimonials, "how to get more bookings" educational content |

### Tier 2/3 Pricing Consideration

| Question                                          | Consideration                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Should we offer a lower-priced tier for Tier 2/3? | Maybe — a "Starter" plan at ₹500/mo for < 10 rooms?                                                                   |
| Or keep the same 3 tiers?                         | Simpler to manage, but ₹1K/mo might be a barrier for very small hotels                                                |
| **Recommendation**                                | Keep 3 tiers but offer a 6-month introductory discount for Tier 2/3: ₹500/mo for first 6 months on Basic, then ₹1K/mo |

## OPEN QUESTIONS FOR DISCUSSION

1. Thailand: own entity or local partner? What are the legal/tax implications?
2. Do we have anyone on the team who speaks Thai?
3. Product localization: how much engineering effort for Thai language + local payments?
4. Tier 2/3 India: do we need a separate "Starter" plan or is Basic at ₹1K sufficient?
5. Field sales for Tier 2/3: hire or use a channel partner network?
6. Mobile-first: is our current product usable on mobile, or does it need significant work?
7. Regional languages: machine translation + human review, or fully manual?
8. Budget for Thailand setup (legal, hiring, marketing): what's the ceiling?
9. Which Tier 2/3 cities do we already have customers in? Can they be references?
10. YouTube/WhatsApp marketing: do we have the content creation capability?
